Thinking about how humans and AI actually collaborate.

Not humans giving orders and AI executing — but how two different ways of thinking
can see each other without one side's judgment being shaped or diluted by the other.

No standard answer to this, so I think and build in parallel:

**[Human-RLAIF (Paper)](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=6928698)** — Human Reinforcement Learning from AI Feedback
Proposes that humans are also being trained by AI: a longitudinal self-case study
on three years of GPT conversations, examining how AI feedback reshapes
questioning strategies, judgment frameworks, and identity coordinates.

**[FlowGrid](https://github.com/dlxeva/FlowGrid)** — Project memory protocol
Keeps decisions and rationale in files, traceable by both humans and AI.

**[biz-retro-analyzer](https://github.com/dlxeva/biz-retro-analyzer)** — Business retro analysis
Turns messy conversations into facts, claims, motives, and next actions.
